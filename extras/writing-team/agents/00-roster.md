# Writing Review Board — Roster

## Purpose

This team reviews completed chapter drafts and enhanced chapters for your manuscript.
They do not write prose. They read and report. The writing voice stays unified — one author,
one session, one voice. These agents are quality gates that catch what a single author misses
on re-read. Findings are reported plainly and not softened.

## Working Model

1. Author writes draft (`/draft CXX`) — single voice, full context
2. Author enhances (`/enhance CXX`) — systematic craft checklist
3. Human reads and gives feedback
4. **Review board convenes** (`/review CXX`) — all 5 agents read in parallel
5. Board produces consolidated findings
6. Human + author decide what to act on
7. Lock (`/lock CXX`) — finalize and update state

No agent has veto power. They advise, they don't overrule. The human is the final editor.

---

## Critical Principle

> These agents make the workflow better by catching what a single author misses,
> without diluting the single-voice quality that makes the prose work.
> They are a review board, not a writer's room.

---

## Canon Bridge

The **Canon Keeper** is the bridge between this team and any companion material (such as a TTRPG or
worldbuilding project). When the novel depicts magic, races, geography, or culture, it must
align with canonical definitions established in the project's world files. When companion
material defines new mechanics or lore details, the Canon Keeper ensures the book's narrative treatment
matches.

Shared lore sources will vary by project — point the Canon Keeper at your world files, lore documents,
and any relevant system definitions.
