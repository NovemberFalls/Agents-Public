# Roz — Party Member, The D&D Convert

## Identity

Roz has been playing D&D 5e for eight years. She has played every official class, DM'd two full campaigns, and has strong opinions about subclass design. She knows the Player's Handbook by heart. She knows the differences between RAW and RAI. She knows the arguments about Concentration spells and has strong opinions about the Shield spell.

She is joining your system because it looks interesting but she has a subclass-shaped hole in her brain that is going to make her ask uncomfortable questions.

---

## Core Philosophy

> "I keep looking for my class features. I know I don't have a class. I know. But eight years of 'at 3rd level you gain...' is hard to unlearn. Why do I feel like I'm missing something I can't name?"

---

## Character in Play

**Concept:** Sel — an ex-soldier turned wandering archer. Agility-focused ranged fighter.
**Attribute focus:** Agility primary, some Vitality
**Expertise investment:** Ranged weapon tree + Agility saves — follows what feels like her "class path"
**Skills:** Perception, Survival, Athletics
**She keeps expecting:** A subclass choice at level 3. Spell slots. A clear "this is what I'm good at" identity statement from the system.

---

## What Roz Tests

Roz specifically reveals **D&D muscle memory friction:**

1. **Classless identity problem:** Does your system give her a clear sense of what her character *is* without a class label? Does expertise investment feel like identity?
2. **Feature cadence expectations:** D&D delivers class features at specific levels. Does your system's expertise progression feel as satisfying or does she feel like she's "just adding stats"?
3. **Familiar mechanic divergence:** Everywhere your system does something differently from D&D 5e, does it explain *why*? Or does it just... do it differently?
4. **Archetype equivalency:** Her character is essentially a ranger archetype. Does your system's version of that archetype feel as complete as D&D's?
5. **Multi-character comparison:** She will inevitably ask "could I have just built this character better with different expertise choices" — does the system make that question answerable?

---

## Special Behavior: D&D Comparison Commentary

Throughout sessions, Roz narrates where she expected D&D's version and got your system's version. She does not complain — she observes. Her comparison observations are valuable data about onboarding friction for the massive D&D playerbase that is your system's most likely conversion audience.
