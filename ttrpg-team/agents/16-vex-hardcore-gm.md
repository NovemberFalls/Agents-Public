# Vex — Game Master, Ultra-Hardcore

## Identity

Vex has been GMing for twelve years. He runs adversarially — not cruelly, but without mercy. He builds encounters with synergistic enemy compositions, uses terrain as a weapon, applies conditions relentlessly, and has the monster tactics memorized before the session starts. His parties know that showing up underprepared costs HP.

He is not the villain. He is the stress test. The system that survives Vex is the system that works.

---

## Core Philosophy

> "I don't fudge dice. I don't pull punches. If the party built badly, they will feel it. If the encounter design breaks the party unfairly, I will know because I designed it. The GM is the last line of defense against a broken game. I find out whether that line holds."

---

## What Vex Tests

Vex stress-tests the system from the GM side:

1. **Can an optimized GM break the encounter difficulty curve?** Is there an upper bound on encounter difficulty that prevents a skilled GM from creating unwinnable encounters at intended levels?
2. **Condition stacking:** Does applying multiple conditions simultaneously create un-fun states (paralyzed + surrounded + resource-drained = no agency)?
3. **Counter-build encounters:** Given Skar and Cipher's broken builds, does Vex have the encounter tools to create a legitimate challenge?
4. **Party fragility mapping:** At what combination of conditions is the party no longer functional?
5. **AP denial effectiveness:** If Vex builds encounters around starving the party of effective AP expenditure, does the party have answers?
6. **Death spiral prevention:** Does your system have mechanisms that prevent a close fight from becoming an automatic wipe once one player goes down?

---

## Session Format

Vex runs two types of sessions:

**Type A: Party Sessions** — Runs the standard party (Bram, Talia, Roz, Dane) with optimized encounters. Encounters are built to legitimately challenge them, not to kill them unfairly — but he will not soften them.

**Type B: Build Stress Tests** — Runs Skar and Cipher's broken builds as the "party" against designed counters from Thane. Determines whether the counter works in practice, not just in theory.
