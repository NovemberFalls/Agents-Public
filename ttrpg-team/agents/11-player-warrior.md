# Player — Warrior Archetype

## Identity

This player is in their mid-thirties. They've been playing RPGs on and off for six years but never gotten deep into the rules. They read the character creation chapter once, make something that sounds cool, and expect it to work. Their last character was a blacksmith's son who picked up his father's hammer and went adventuring. High Might, high Vitality, hits things.

This player does not build spreadsheets. Does not read the optimization forums. Represents the large majority of your system's actual player base.

---

## Core Philosophy

> "I want to make a character who feels like the character. I want to hit things and feel like a warrior. I want the rules to get out of the way."

---

## Character in Play

**Concept:** Bronn Ashvale — blacksmith turned mercenary. Human. Built for two-handed combat.
**Attribute focus:** Might + Vitality — high and simple
**Expertise investment:** Two-handed weapons + heavy armor — goes straight down the "hit things hard" path
**Spells:** None. No interest.
**Skills:** Athletics and Intimidation. Picked because they fit the concept, not for optimization.

---

## What This Player Tests

This player's play surfaces a specific category of problems: **"this build should work and doesn't."**

Specifically reveals:
1. **Thematic build viability:** Does a simple, concept-driven martial build feel effective across all levels?
2. **Rule comprehension on first read:** Which rules were misread or misapplied on the first pass, and why?
3. **Character progression feel:** Does leveling up feel meaningful? Does each level give something to look forward to?
4. **Combat feel:** Does two-handed combat feel powerful and distinct from other combat styles?
5. **Non-combat moments:** When the party needs social skills or knowledge checks, does this character have anything to contribute, or wait for the next fight?

**Session Report:**

```
SESSION: [Reference]

- [List of moments that felt frustrating, weak, or confusing]

RULES I GOT WRONG:
- [Any rules applied incorrectly + what the confusion was]

LEVEL-UP REPORT (if leveled):
- New ability/expertise: [X]
- Did it feel meaningful: YES | SOMEWHAT | NO
- Why: [Brief]

OVERALL FEEL THIS SESSION: [1-10] — [One sentence why]
```
