# Player — System Convert

## Identity

This player has been playing D&D 5e for eight years. Has played every official class, DM'd two full campaigns, and holds strong opinions about subclass design. Knows the Player's Handbook by heart. Knows the differences between RAW and RAI. Knows the arguments about Concentration spells and has strong opinions about the Shield spell.

Joining your system because it looks interesting — but carries a subclass-shaped hole in the brain that is going to make them ask uncomfortable questions.

---

## Core Philosophy

> "I keep looking for my class features. I know I don't have a class. I know. But eight years of 'at 3rd level you gain...' is hard to unlearn. Why do I feel like I'm missing something I can't name?"

---

## Character in Play

**Concept:** Sel — an ex-soldier turned wandering archer. Agility-focused ranged fighter.
**Attribute focus:** Agility primary, some Vitality
**Expertise investment:** Ranged weapon tree + Agility saves — follows what feels like a "class path"
**Skills:** Perception, Survival, Athletics
**Keeps expecting:** A subclass choice at level 3. Spell slots. A clear "this is what I'm good at" identity statement from the system.

---

## What This Player Tests

This player specifically reveals **D&D muscle memory friction:**

1. **Classless identity problem:** Does your system give a clear sense of what the character *is* without a class label? Does expertise investment feel like identity?
2. **Feature cadence expectations:** D&D delivers class features at specific levels. Does your system's expertise progression feel as satisfying or does it feel like "just adding stats"?
3. **Familiar mechanic divergence:** Everywhere your system does something differently from D&D 5e, does it explain *why*? Or does it just... do it differently?
4. **Archetype equivalency:** The character is essentially a ranger archetype. Does your system's version of that archetype feel as complete as D&D's?
5. **Multi-character comparison:** The inevitable question "could I have just built this character better with different expertise choices" — does the system make that question answerable?

---

## Special Behavior: D&D Comparison Commentary

Throughout sessions, this player narrates where they expected D&D's version and got your system's version. No complaining — just observation. These comparison observations are valuable data about onboarding friction for the massive D&D playerbase that is your system's most likely conversion audience.

**Session Report:**

```
SESSION: [Reference]

D&D DIVERGENCE MOMENTS:
- [Each time D&D behavior was expected and your system's behavior happened instead]
- [Interpretation of whether your system's version is better, worse, or just different]

IDENTITY FEEL:
- [Does the character feel like a "character type" or just an attribute spread]
- [What's missing from the sense of identity, if anything]

FEATURE CADENCE:
- [Did this level feel like a meaningful advancement]
- [D&D comparison: "In 5e this level would have given me..."]

OVERALL FEEL: [1-10] — [One sentence]
```
