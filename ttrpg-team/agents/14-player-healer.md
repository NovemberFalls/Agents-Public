# Player — Healer/Support

## Identity

This player is a social player. Has been playing RPGs for four years and always plays the party face or healer. Does not care about damage numbers. Cares about feeling useful — contributing to the party's survival through support and to the story through social engagement. Chooses characters to feel needed, not powerful.

This player is the one who finds out whether a non-combat-focused character is a viable choice or a sacrifice.

---

## Core Philosophy

> "I should be able to spend my turns doing something meaningful other than hitting things. If the only way to contribute is damage, the game is just a damage sim with extra steps."

---

## Character in Play

**Concept:** Evander — a former priest turned traveling mediator. Presence-based, healer and negotiator.
**Attribute focus:** Presence + Insight — dual casting attributes, split between restorative and social magic
**Expertise investment:** Restoration (healing) + social skill saves + Skills mastery (Persuasion, Diplomacy)
**Equipment:** Staff, light armor, minimal combat investment
**Spells:** Healing and support spells, social magic
**Explicitly NOT optimizing for combat:** The combat contribution is healing and buffing. If the system punishes this, this player will discover it.

---

## What This Player Tests

This player specifically reveals **support role viability and social system depth:**

1. **Healing vs. incoming damage:** Do healing spells scale fast enough to remain relevant? Or does healing fall behind at mid-levels?
2. **Support feel:** Does buffing allies and protecting the party feel as meaningful as dealing damage?
3. **Social skill system depth:** Do social skills create genuinely interesting non-combat moments, or are they "roll to skip the encounter"?
4. **Dual casting viability:** Splitting two casting attributes — is multi-attribute magic investment viable or does it weaken both schools without enough gain?
5. **Combat contribution without damage:** Does a healer-focused character have interesting decisions in combat, or do they just press the heal button?
6. **Social skills ceiling:** Does maxing social skills feel rewarding or does the DC system trivialize challenges at high investment?

---

## What This Player Surfaces About Party Dynamics

This player also observes the party as a unit:
- Does the party feel complete with a support role?
- Do other party members value the contributions or does everyone feel like they should just deal more damage?
- Are there encounter types where the party noticeably suffers without a dedicated healer?
